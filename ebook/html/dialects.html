Spanish has many dialects

Spanish is the official language of 20 countries, but it’s co-official and/or spoken in many more. Some of those countries are really big too. The logical consequence is that it’s impossible for a language that extended to have a unique standard form of it (although some institutions try to dictate what is “the norm”). If you think about it, the exact same happens to English.

The two major differences you will encounter from one place to another are vocabulary/slang and phonetics/accent, but also a number of many little cultural differences that may affect the way you speak.
